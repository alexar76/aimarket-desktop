## Case 1: The Independent Developer — "No one sells ATS rules for Greenhouse"

### Background

Maria is a freelance developer specializing in HR tech integrations. She's been looking for a side project that has clear market demand but low competition. She opens Discovery Prospector for the first time.

### Step-by-Step

1. **Launch** — Maria starts the Flutter desktop app and connects to `hub.aicom.io` with her wallet key (a $5 channel is opened automatically).

2. **Dashboard loads** — The main dashboard shows underserved niches ranked by `niche_score`. She filters by category `career`:

   | Rank | Niche | Demand Score | Supply Count | Niche Score |
   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | 1 | ATS rules for Greenhouse | 0.87 | 0 | 0.81 |
   | 2 | Resume scoring for fintech roles | 0.72 | 2 | 0.65 |
   | 3 | Cover letter generation for senior roles | 0.61 | 4 | 0.52 |

3. **Inspect gap** — Maria clicks the top row. The detail panel shows:
   - 342 monthly searches for "ATS scoring Greenhouse"
   - 89 purchase attempts that returned zero results
   - Estimated monthly market: $4,104
   - Suggested capability type: `scoring-function`
   - Example input schema provided

4. **Copy SDK code** — Maria clicks "Copy SDK Code" and gets a ready-to-paste snippet:

   ```dart
   // generated by Discovery Prospector
   final agent = AimarketAgent(
     hubUrl: 'https://hub.aicom.io',
     walletKey: walletKey,
   );
   final channel = await agent.openChannel(5.00);

   // Invoke the ATS scoring capability Maria will build
   final result = await agent.invoke(
     capabilityId: 'my-ats-scoring-greenhouse',
     input: {
       'candidate_profile': candidateProfile,
       'job_description': jobDescription,
       'ats_system': 'greenhouse',
     },
     channelId: channel.id,
   );
   ```

5. **Build and list** — Maria builds the capability, publishes it on the hub, and sets a price of $0.15 per call. She's the first mover in this niche.

6. **Result** — Within 30 days, Maria's capability serves 200+ invocations, earning ~$30 in residual income. The hub now has supply for a previously underserved niche.

## Case 2: The Data Scientist — Identifying High-Demand, Low-Supply Categories

### Background

Javid is a data scientist at a marketplace analytics firm. His clients want to know which AI capability categories are most underserved. He uses Discovery Prospector's SDK programmatically to build a category-level heatmap.

### Step-by-Step

1. **Batch pull** — Javid writes a Dart script that pulls telemetry for all top-level categories:

   ```dart
   for (final category in ['career', 'finance', 'health', 'legal', 'education']) {
     final telemetry = await agent.invoke(
       capabilityId: 'marketplace-telemetry-aggregated',
       input: {'category': category, 'period': '90d'},
       channelId: channel.id,
     );
     // Store for analysis
   }
   ```

2. **Cross-category analysis** — Javid computes aggregated metrics:

   | Category | Average Demand | Avg Supply | Avg Gap Score | Underserved Sub-Categories |
   |---|---|---|---|---|
   | career | 0.74 | 8 | 0.63 | 12 |
   | finance | 0.61 | 15 | 0.48 | 7 |
   | health | 0.82 | 3 | 0.71 | 18 |
   | legal | 0.55 | 20 | 0.35 | 4 |
   | education | 0.68 | 6 | 0.58 | 9 |

3. **Identify opportunity** — Health has the highest gap score (0.71) with only 3 capabilities across all sub-categories. Telemedicine documentation and medical coding are the most underserved sub-niches.

4. **Report** — Javid publishes a report: "Health AI capabilities represent the largest underserved opportunity in the marketplace, with an estimated $12K/month unmet demand."

5. **Impact** — Three developer teams use Javid's report to prioritize health-focused capabilities. Within two months, health category supply grows from 3 to 14 capabilities.

## Case 3: The Marketplace Curator — Prioritizing Capability Seeds

### Background

Aisha is the marketplace curator for the aicom hub. Her job is to decide which "seed" capabilities the hub should build internally (via grants or bounties) to bootstrap supply. She uses Discovery Prospector to make data-driven prioritization decisions.

### Step-by-Step

1. **Full pull** — Aisha pulls 90-day telemetry across all categories with maximum granularity.

2. **Filter by hub strategic goals** — She filters gaps to only those in categories the hub has identified as strategic: `career`, `finance`, and `legal`.

3. **Score and rank** — The gaps are sorted by a weighted score that includes the hub's strategic alignment factor (1.5x for strategic categories):

   | Priority | Niche | Strategic | Gap Score | Weighted | Est. Monthly Market |
   |---|---|---|---|---|---|
   | 1 | ATS rules for Greenhouse | Yes (career) | 0.81 | 1.22 | $4,104 |
   | 2 | Mortgage pre-qual scoring | Yes (finance) | 0.74 | 1.11 | $3,820 |
   | 3 | Contract clause extraction | Yes (legal) | 0.62 | 0.93 | $2,450 |
   | 4 | Learning path generation | No (education) | 0.58 | 0.58 | $1,900 |

4. **Seed decision** — Aisha allocates grant budget to build capability seeds for the top 3 niches:

   - **Greenhouse ATS Rules** — $500 bounty, estimated 4 weeks to build
   - **Mortgage Pre-Qual Scoring** — $750 bounty, estimated 6 weeks to build
   - **Contract Clause Extraction** — $600 bounty, estimated 5 weeks to build

5. **Track results** — Aisha monitors the seeded capabilities' performance over 90 days:
   - All three reach 100+ invocations/month within 60 days
   - Two of the three seed builders continue maintaining and updating their capabilities
   - The Greenhouse niche attracts 3 additional competitive capabilities within 90 days

6. **Outcome** — Data-driven seeding reduces the hub's "dead grant" rate from 40% to 12%. The marketplace supply grows 3x faster in seeded categories versus unseeded ones.
