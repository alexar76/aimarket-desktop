# Personal Finance Coach — User Cases

## Case 1: Individual Tracking Spending

**User:** Sarah, 32, product designer in Austin, TX

**Situation:** Has 3 bank accounts, 2 credit cards. Uses CSV exports monthly. Wants to know where her money goes without uploading to Mint/YNAB.

**Flow:**
1. Imports 5 CSVs into Finance Coach
2. App auto-detects column mappings, parses 847 transactions
3. Opens Marketplace tab, buys "US-TX tax rules 2026" for $0.25 and "ML categorizer v3" for $0.10
4. Categorizer runs locally, tags transactions: 32% rent, 18% food, 12% transport, 8% subscriptions
5. Dashboard shows: "You spend 8% on subscriptions — cohort average in Austin is 5%"
6. Opts in to share anonymized pattern, earns $0.50 credit

**Outcome:** Full financial picture without uploading a single transaction to the cloud.

## Case 2: Freelancer Doing Tax Estimation

**User:** Marco, freelance developer in Berlin

**Situation:** Needs quarterly tax estimate. German tax rules are complex and change yearly. Doesn't want to pay a Steuerberater 400 EUR.

**Flow:**
1. Imports yearly bank CSV (1,200 transactions)
2. Buys "DE freelancer tax rules 2026" from marketplace — $0.25
3. Buys "DE VAT categorization rules" — $0.10
4. Local tax engine applies rules: estimates 8,400 EUR quarterly payment
5. Categorizes 89% of transactions automatically into tax-relevant buckets
6. Exports tax estimate PDF for Steuerberater review

**Outcome:** Tax estimate in 10 minutes for $0.35 instead of hours of manual work or 400 EUR consultation.

## Case 3: Couple Planning Joint Budget

**User:** Alex & Jordan, planning to merge finances after marriage

**Situation:** Both have separate accounts. Want to see combined picture before opening joint account. Privacy concern: don't want joint financial data in any cloud service.

**Flow:**
1. Each imports their CSVs into separate profiles
2. App computes combined view locally — no data leaves either device
3. Buys "couple budgeting benchmarks 2026" from marketplace — $0.50
4. Benchmark shows: "couples in your age bracket in Seattle allocate 35% to housing, you're at 42%"
5. Uses what-if simulator: "if we move to a 30% housing situation, we save $8,400/year"

**Outcome:** Data-driven joint budget decision without sharing financial data with any third party.
